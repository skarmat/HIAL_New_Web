The story of HIAL started when His Holiness Drikyung Skyabgon Chetsang Rinpochey, one of the top leaders in Tibetan Buddhism and UN ambassador for mountain partnership, met with Sonam Wangchuk, the founder of the SECMOL alternative school. Sonam requested His Holiness to help the Himalayan region in establishing an alternative university that would engage mountain youth in studies related to their own unique environment and needs. His Holiness gave his blessings and thus began the incredible journey of HIAL that we are witnessing today.

“Learning by Doing: HIAL’s experiential learning programs encourage learner development through Live Learning Labs and self-governance models that supplement the traditional academic setting, instilling qualities of leadership, cultural awareness, and entrepreneurship.”

HIAL Conceptual Model

4 Key Pillars

“Learners of all academic levels are encouraged to undertake research and creative endeavors in collaboration with facilitators to foster free-thinking, and help in evolving solutions to address key challenges experienced by mountain communities."

“HIAL is a collaborative exercise between learners and facilitators, and aims to inspire the seekers of knowledge to explore both outside and within themselves. Instead of solving problems with the conventional 3Rs (Reading, wRiting, and aRithmentic), we encourage learners to problem solve using the 3Hs: Bright Head, Kind Heart, and Skilled Hands.”

Sonam Wangchuk, Founding Director
